Paella

The Paella comes from Valecia, Spain. The Paella is cooked in a big frying pan served with yellow rice, seafood and peppers. The Paella can chiken instead of seafood for the people that dont like seafood. If you want to challenge yourself you can have crab or octopus on your paella.

Green Juice

The green juice is juice with fruits and green vegetables like spinaca, cucumber and lettuce. For fruits theres green apple, lemon, pinnaple, mango and orange. Green juice can give you chlorophyll (aka liquid sunshine.) Chlorophyll strengthens your immune system, helps control inflammation, increases red blood cell production, and helps enhance your cells' ability to carry oxygen.

Chocolate Smash Hearts, are basically a chocolate case shaped in the form of a heart, that comes with a cavity to be filled in with candies, cakes, chocolates, non edible gifts just about anything. This is then smashed using a hammer to reveal the surprise inside just like a regular pinata cake.

Arepa

The arepa is a flat, round, unleavened patty of soaked, ground kernels of maize, or—more frequently nowadays—maize meal or maize flour that can be grilled, baked, fried, boiled or steamed (AKA a sandwich). Arepas can have anything Fish, Ham, Chicken, Meat, Chesse etc.

Ceviche (Green Sea Version)

Ceviche is a healthy dish that is usually served as an appetizer. It’s generally made from fish or shrimp, that’s marinated in lemon and/or lime citrus juices. The acidity in the citrus cures the fish causing it to denature the proteins and become firm and opaque while absorbing flavor.

Vuelve a la Vida

Vuelve a la Vida (Come back to life) is a seafood preparation native to Venezuela and its coasts. It is due to the popular belief of the aphrodisiac properties that seafood has in this presentation. It also looks like a brain.

Salpicon

Salpicón de marisco or salpicón de marisco venezolano is a delicious fresh salad of fish, seafood and vegetables such as peppers, onion and tomato. Salpicón originates from Spain, it once seemed to mean a cold dish spiced with vinegar but the elements of which are cooked. Aromatic herbs can complement the seasoning, usually cilantro, chives or parsley. Salpicón is always eaten very fresh.

Chesse Boards

A cheeseboard (or cheese course) is served at the end of a meal before or following dessert, or replacing the last course. A cheeseboard typically has contrasting cheeses with accompaniments, such as crackers, biscuits, fruits, nuts, celery or candy.
